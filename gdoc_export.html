Pitch


HTML5 In the Real World: Lessons from 5 years of building HTML5 games



Description:

With support growing considerably over the past 5 years, today many of the top grossing games on iOS and Android are built with HTML5. Adam Proctor and Owen Lawson, lead developers with Uken Games, go through the key lessons they've learned when it comes to building successful cross-platform games. With stories from the front lines of web based game development, they will talk about the strengths and weaknesses of modern mobile browsers, pitfalls to be avoided and where the technology is going. Focusing on real examples from live HTML5 games, Owen and Adam will highlight what has worked, what hasn't and where there's still room for growth.



Tell Us More:



HTML5 is a popular topic of conversation in the developer community, but there is a scarcity of examples of it’s use in the real world. Most talks on HTML5 in games are focused on the viability of the technology, and are theoretical about it’s future use. This talk will instead focus on an open and frank discussion of the use of HTML5 in currently live and successful games. This is a technical talk intended for developers.

  • 2 - Preloading assets is HARD / Browser’s are getting too smart - Adam

    • iOS ignores prefetch

    • memory leaks using background-image, but <img> tags will not be loaded, even if they’re visible, if the device is low on memory

    • images loaded in 1024x1024 chunks on iOS
